About This Project

What This Is

Affordability for All is a strategic communications framework developed during a Coro Southern California Fellowship placement with Mayor Karen Bass's Office of Policy and Communications. The goal: take 209 programs across 20+ departments and build a unified narrative architecture that any Angeleno, press contact, or field representative can understand in a single conversation.

The Five Fights framework organizes those programs into five constituent-facing categories: Earn More, Build More, Save More, Care More, and Recover More. Each fight maps to a real economic pressure Angelenos face and is backed by concrete programs, dollar figures, and measurable outcomes.

The Scoring Rubric

Programs are scored on a 14-point rubric across five criteria: Bass Attribution, Scale of Impact, Communications Value, Strategic Alignment with the Affordability Agenda, and Implementation Reality. Two binary flags — Equity Depth and Durability — are noted but do not affect the score.

  • A-List: 12+ points — Bass's signature programs with the highest public recognition and impact
  • Tier 1: 9–11 points — High-impact programs with strong Bass attribution
  • Tier 2: 5–8 points — Solid programmatic work with moderate communications potential
  • Tier 3: Below 5 — Infrastructure, passthroughs, or programs with limited public legibility
